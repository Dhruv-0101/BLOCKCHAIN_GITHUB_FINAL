Chapter 01

💰 What is a Stablecoin?

A stablecoin is a cryptocurrency designed to stay stable in value, usually pegged to something like the US Dollar.

✅

1 USDT ≈ 1 USD

Tether — the largest stablecoin by market cap, always tracks the US dollar.

✅

1 USDC ≈ 1 USD

USD Coin by Circle — fully backed by cash and US Treasury bonds.

🧠 Simple Definition

Stablecoin = Digital money on the blockchain that doesn't fluctuate like normal crypto. It's the "calm" asset in a sea of volatility.

🎯 Why Do Stablecoins Exist? (The Real Need)

Let's understand the problem first.

⚠️

Problem: Crypto is wildly volatile

You buy Bitcoin at $60,000. Next day → $55,000. Next week → $65,000.
That's great for profit, but terrible for stability if you want to use money in everyday life.

💡

Solution: Stablecoin

Stablecoins give you a way to say: "I want to stay inside crypto, but I don't want price risk right now."

Chapter 03

🔗 What Does "Pegged" Mean?

Pegged = Fixed to a reference value. A stablecoin is pegged to USD, meaning 1 coin ≈ 1 USD — always trying to stay equal.

🎮

The Game Zone Analogy

You go to a game zone and give ₹1000. You get 1000 game tokens. 1 token = ₹1 (fixed). No matter what happens — you can always exchange 1 token back for ₹1. That fixed relationship = the PEG.

📊 How the Peg Plays Out in Practice

✅

Scenario 1: Perfect Peg

$1.00

Market price = $1. Everything balanced.

📉

Scenario 2: Peg Drops

$0.98

Arbitrage traders buy at $0.98, redeem for $1. Demand rises → peg restored.

📈

Scenario 3: Peg Rises

$1.02

Traders mint new coins for $1, sell at $1.02. Supply increases → peg restored.

🧠 Deep Intuition

The peg is NOT magic. It works because of three things: Incentives (profit opportunities for arbitrageurs), Trust (people believe the backing exists), and Mechanisms (the ability to mint/redeem). Remove any one of these, and the peg starts to wobble.

Chapter 04

🧩 Types of Stablecoins

How a stablecoin maintains its $1 peg is what truly differentiates it. There are several approaches — each with different trade-offs.

1️⃣

Fiat-Backed (Most Common) — Backed/Anchored

🟢 Safer

Example: USDT, USDC

For every 1 coin issued, $1 is stored in a bank (or equivalent assets like Treasury bonds). You give $1000 → you get 1000 USDC. Return 1000 USDC → you get $1000 back.

🎫

Cloakroom Ticket Analogy

You give your bag (real money) to the cloakroom. They give you a token. The token is proof of your real asset — always redeemable. USDT = that token.

2️⃣

Crypto-Backed (Over-Collateralized)

🟡 Medium

Example: DAI (by MakerDAO)

Instead of dollars, you lock crypto (like ETH). But because crypto is volatile, you must lock more than what you borrow.

Lock $150 worth of ETH → Get $100 DAI // Over-collateralization protects against price swings Ratio: 150% minimum collateral
3️⃣

Algorithmic (Risky)

🔴 High Risk

Example: TerraUSD (now failed)

No real backing — stability comes purely from code + supply control. If price > $1: increase supply. If price < $1: decrease supply.

🌡️

The Thermostat Analogy

Too hot → cool it down. Too cold → heat it up. No real backing — just a control system. Works only if people trust it. If trust breaks → 💥 collapse.

⚠️ The TerraUSD Crash (2022)

Lost its peg and collapsed to near $0. A stark reminder: stablecoin ≠ always safe. The peg depends on system strength, not just the name.

4️⃣

Governance-Based (DAO-Controlled)

🟡 Medium

Example: DAI (MakerDAO)

Controlled by a DAO (community voting) instead of a company. Users vote on interest rates, collateral rules, and risk parameters.

🏢

Society Building Analogy

No single owner. Residents (token holders) vote on all the rules. The system runs by community consensus — not a CEO.

⚖️ Exogenous vs Endogenous Collateral

This is one of the most important distinctions in DeFi safety:

🟢

Exogenous Collateral

Backing comes from OUTSIDE the system. Example: DAI backed by ETH. Even if DAI has issues, ETH still has its own market value. The backup doesn't depend on the system itself.

Lock ETH (external) → Get DAI ETH ≠ DAI → Safer ✅

🧠 "My backing doesn't depend on me."

🔴

Endogenous Collateral

Backing comes from INSIDE the same system. Example: TerraUSD backed by LUNA. Both tokens are in the same ecosystem — if one falls, the other falls too.

LUNA (internal) backs UST If UST falls → LUNA also falls 💥

🧠 "If I fall, my backup also falls."

Chapter 07

🔒 Understanding Collateral & Ratios

🔒 What is Collateral? (Quick Feel)

Collateral = a backup asset you lock to create/borrow a stablecoin. Like: you give something valuable → you get money in return. You hold on to your loan as long as the backup is good enough.

❗ Common Misconception

"System jitna dega usse jyada user dega… like 50% more always?"

Partly correct, but NOT always fixed at 50%. The system doesn't demand a fixed extra amount — it demands a minimum safety ratio, which varies by protocol.

🧠 The Real Concept: Collateral Ratio (CR)

Different protocols set different minimum ratios:

CollateralLoanRatioSafety
$150$100150% Minimum Safe
$200$100200% Safer
$120$100120% Dangerous!

⚡ One-Line Correction

❌ "Always 50% extra"
✅ "Always above a minimum required ratio."

The system says: "I don't want a fixed amount — I want safety. Give me enough buffer so I'm not exposed."

Why Is More Collateral Better?

Because prices can fall. Example: $150 collateral drops to $120 → still safe at 120% ratio. $200 collateral drops to $120 → even safer. The extra buffer = breathing room for price crashes.

Chapter 08 — The Full Story

🌟 The DeFi Odyssey: Alice & Bob

Let's walk through the entire lifecycle of a crypto-backed stablecoin system. Think of it as a Digital Pawn Shop — you put up gold (ETH), take a loan (SSC), and if things go bad, a bounty hunter (Bob) cleans it up.

📜 The Full Scenario at a Glance

Alice's Starting State: ETH held: 2 ETH ETH Price: $2,500 Collateral Value: $5,000 Loan (SSC minted): $2,000 Health Factor: (5000 × 0.5) / 2000 = 1.25 ✅ SAFE After Market Crash (ETH → $1,800): Collateral Value: 2 × 1800 = $3,600 Health Factor: (3600 × 0.5) / 2000 = 0.9 🚨 DANGER
1

⚔️ The Rules — How This Bank Works

This system has two strict rules to keep everything safe:

The Gold Standard

You can only borrow money if you deposit $2 worth of Gold (ETH) for every $1 you borrow. This is the 50% Liquidation Threshold — meaning your collateral must always be ≥2× your debt.

The Penalty

If your Gold's value drops and fails the 2:1 ratio, any "Hunter" can step in, pay your debt, and take your Gold at a discount (with a 10% bonus for their trouble).

2

☀️ Alice Enters — The Golden Days

Alice has 2 ETH. ETH Price is $2,500/ETH. Total Wealth: $5,000.

Alice mints (borrows) $2,000 SSC.

// Health Factor Calculation Collateral Value = 2 ETH × $2,500 = $5,000 Adjusted Collateral = $5,000 × 50% = $2,500 Health Factor = $2,500 / $2,000 = 1.25 ✅ // The Bank says: "You could borrow up to $2,500. You only took $2,000. SAFE!"
3

📉 The Storm — Market Crash

Suddenly, ETH crashes from $2,500 → $1,800.

New ETH Price = $1,800 Alice's Gold Value = 2 ETH × $1,800 = $3,600 Adjusted Collateral = $3,600 × 50% = $1,800 Health Factor = $1,800 / $2,000 = 0.9 🚨 DANGER! // "Alice, your debt should not exceed $1,800, but you owe $2,000!" // Health Factor below 1.0 → Account is now open for LIQUIDATION

Alice's account is now under-collateralized. Any bounty hunter in the world can now liquidate her position and earn a profit.

4

🐺 Bob the Hunter — The Liquidation

Enter Bob. He doesn't care about Alice. He only cares about profit. He sees Alice owes $2,000 but has $3,600 worth of ETH sitting there.

Bob's Move: Pay the system: $1,000 SSC (clears half Alice's debt) Bob's Reward: ETH worth $1,000 + 10% bonus ($100) = $1,100 worth of ETH How much ETH? $1,000 / $1,800 (ETH price) = 0.555 ETH + 10% bonus = 0.055 ETH Total ETH to Bob = 0.611 ETH ✅ Bob's Profit: Spent $1,000 → Received $1,100 worth of ETH Net Profit: +$100 in a single transaction 🎉
5

🕊️ Recovery — The New Normal

Alice's Account After Liquidation: Remaining Debt = $2,000 - $1,000 = $1,000 Remaining ETH = $3,600 - $1,100 = $2,500 (in ETH terms ≈ 1.389 ETH) New Health Factor: Adjusted Collateral = $2,500 × 50% = $1,250 Health Factor = $1,250 / $1,000 = 1.25 ✅ SAFE AGAIN!

Why is Alice okay?

Her account wasn't wiped out. She lost a ~$100 "fine" (Bob's bonus), but her remaining $2,500 in ETH is now safely collateralizing her $1,000 debt.

Why is the System happy?

Alice's "bad debt" was removed. The stablecoin (SSC) stayed fully backed by enough collateral. No insolvency. No peg break.

Chapter 10 — Questions Answered

🤔 Real Questions, Real Answers

Q
Yaha pe to dekho system ko to loss ho hi nahi raha hai — suppose ETH = $1,800 to 2 ETH girvi the to $3,600 worth hai abhi system ke pass and usne user ko diya hai $2,000. Agar user bhag bhi gaya to system ko to koi loss hai hi nahi?

You are absolutely right! As long as the ETH price stays above $1,000, the system is technically safe because Alice's 2 ETH are still worth more than her $2,000 debt.

However, the reason we need liquidation and users like Bob is to ensure the system never even gets close to that "Insolvency Line." Think of it like this:

1. The Safety Cushion: The system only gave Alice $2,000 when she deposited $5,000. This $3,000 gap is a cushion. Crypto prices are extremely volatile — the cushion gives the system "room to breathe" if the market drops overnight.

2. The Real Threat — The Flash Crash: Imagine ETH doesn't stop at $1,800. What if it crashes to $900 in a few minutes? Alice's 2 ETH = only $1,800. Alice's debt = $2,000. Now the system is at a loss. Alice "runs away" with $2,000 in her pocket while the system only has $1,800. The stablecoin loses its peg → system goes insolvent (bankrupt).

3. Bob's Role — The Preventive Strike: Bob's job is to ensure we never hit the "Death Line." As soon as the price hits $1,800 (danger zone), Bob starts liquidating. The 10% bonus is Bob's motivation to act fast. Without a bonus, Bob might wait too long — and by the time he acts, ETH might have already fallen below the debt amount.

4. In DeFi, the user is already "gone": There is no KYC. Alice has the $2,000 SSC tokens and can spend them anywhere. The system doesn't care about Alice's identity — it only cares about her ETH collateral. The system relies entirely on Bob to pay back the debt (burning SSC) and clean up the collateral before its value crashes below the debt.

Q
Why does the user lose SO much in liquidation? Feels harsh — pura ETH chala gaya?

I get why this feels harsh. Let's make the "why" super clear with plain logic.

Core truth: The system is built to protect $1 = $1 for the stablecoin. It is NOT built to protect the borrower.

Think like the system: its only rule is "I gave out $100. I must be able to get back $100 right now." Not "User ne pehle $200 diya tha." Not "Let's be fair." Just: "Can I recover $100 today?"

Three things hit together:
1️⃣ Price crash already killed value — ETH went $200 → $90. That $110 loss is market risk YOU took. Not the system's fault.
2️⃣ System must act fast — Crypto falls fast. If it waits: $90 → $70 → $50. Every second of delay = more potential loss.
3️⃣ Liquidation bonus — To attract a liquidator, the system offers extra incentive. That extra comes from your collateral.

🏦

Real-World Bank Analogy

You take a loan, give ₹2000 gold. Gold crashes to ₹900. Bank doesn't wait. It immediately sells your gold, possibly at a discount to attract a quick buyer. You lose the gold completely. The bank didn't do this to hurt you — it did it to survive.

Important shift in thinking: That extra buffer ($100 or $3,000) was not yours to keep safely. It was the system's insurance. You already took your loan. The buffer exists to protect the system, not you.

💥 One-Line Brutal Truth

You borrowed safety using a risky asset. When it failed, you paid the price. The system is not unfair — it's strict by design. That strictness is why the stablecoin stays stable.

System Health Check

Why This Contract Works

Every single operation — deposit, mint, burn, withdraw, liquidate — ends with a call to _revertIfHealthFactorIsBroken(). This single guard ensures that no matter what path a user takes through the system, they can never leave their account in an unsafe state. It's the DeFi equivalent of a circuit breaker — it doesn't prevent bad markets, but it ensures the protocol never holds more debt than collateral.
