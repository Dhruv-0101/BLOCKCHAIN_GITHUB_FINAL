🔥 Uniswap V1 – Complete Deep Explanation

1️⃣ What was Uniswap V1? (Big Picture)

Uniswap V1 (2018) was a decentralized exchange (DEX) that:

  • Does not use an order book
  • No buyer–seller matching
  • Runs purely on smart contracts
  • Introduced the Automated Market Maker (AMM) concept
💡 Meaning:
"Price is not decided by people — the formula decides it."

2️⃣ The Most Important Rule – AMM Formula

The heart ❤️ of Uniswap V1 is:

x * y = k
  • x = ETH reserve
  • y = Token reserve
  • k = constant (does not change after liquidity)
⚠️ This rule must always be maintained

💡 Intuition (to get a feel)

Think of a shop with:

  • 10 ETH
  • 1,000 TOKEN

So:

k = 10 * 1000 = 10,000

Now someone comes:

  • Wants to give 1 ETH
  • Wants to take TOKEN

ETH reserve increases → TOKEN reserve must decrease
So that x*y = 10,000 stays

➡️ That is why as more buying happens, price keeps getting higher

3️⃣ Structure of Uniswap V1

❗ Important Limitation

Each token had a separate exchange contract

Example:

  • ETH ↔ DAI → one contract
  • ETH ↔ USDC → different contract
  • ETH ↔ BAT → different contract
⚠️ Direct Token ↔ Token trading was not possible
Everything happened through ETH.

4️⃣ How Did Trading Work? (ETH ⇌ ERC20)

Case 1️⃣: ETH → TOKEN (Buy Token)

You give ETH, you take token.

Formula (simplified):

tokens_out = (tokens_reserve * eth_in) / (eth_reserve + eth_in)

(After fees, actual amount received is slightly less)

⭐ As eth_in increases:
• price gets worse
• slippage increases

Case 2️⃣: TOKEN → ETH (Sell Token)

You give token, you take ETH.

Formula:

eth_out = (eth_reserve * tokens_in) / (token_reserve + tokens_in)

❗ Fees (IMPORTANT)

  • 0.3% fee on every trade
  • This fee:
    • Uniswap does not keep it
    • It goes to Liquidity Providers (LPs)

5️⃣ How Did Token → Token Trade Happen?

Direct — ✗ NOT POSSIBLE

Example: DAI → USDC

What actually happened:

DAI → ETH → USDC

This means:

  • 2 trades
  • 2x fees
  • 2x slippage 😵

6️⃣ Liquidity Providers (LPs) – Who Puts in the Money?

LP's job:

  • Deposit ETH + Token
  • Give liquidity to the pool
  • Earn fees

Rule:

💡 ETH and Token must be deposited in equal value

Example:

  • ETH price = ₹2,00,000
  • You are adding 1 ETH

So:

  • ₹2,00,000 worth of token must also be added

What does the LP receive?

  • Liquidity Tokens (LP tokens)
  • These represent your share in the pool

7️⃣ Fees Distribution – How Does LP's Profit Form?

  • 0.3% fee on every trade
  • This fee gets added to the pool
  • When LP withdraws:
    • Gets principal + fees
⭐ More trading volume = more LP profit

8️⃣ Impermanent Loss (Most Confusing but IMPORTANT)

In simple language:

If the token's price changes a lot in the outside market
and the pool balance adjusts automatically
then LP gets less money compared to just HODLing

Example:

  • You deposited ETH + TOKEN
  • Token's price went 5x

AMM will:

  • Keep selling token
  • Keep buying ETH

Result:

  • You are left with fewer tokens
  • If you had HODLed, profit would have been more
😮 This is called Impermanent Loss
⚠️ "Impermanent" only until you withdraw
Once you withdraw it becomes permanent loss

9️⃣ Slippage – Why Does Price Go Wrong?

Slippage = Expected price vs Actual price difference

Why does it happen?

  • Pool has low liquidity
  • You are making a large trade
  • x*y=k is forcefully maintained
⭐ In V1, slippage was very high for big trades

🧠 Impermanent Loss – REAL LIFE EXAMPLE (DESI STYLE)

🛒 Example 1: Vegetable Market Example 🥔🍅

Imagine you have an automatic vegetable market.

You put in at the start:

  • 10 potatoes 🥔
  • 10 tomatoes 🍅

Market rate:

  • 1 potato = ₹10
  • 1 tomato = ₹10

Total value = ₹200

💡 Rule of the market:
potato × tomato = constant

📝 What happened now?

In the outside market:

  • Tomato price went from ₹10 → ₹100 😱
  • Potato price remains same ₹10

What will people do?

💡 They will buy tomatoes from the cheaper place

🤖 What will your automatic market do?

Machine says:

"Tomato is becoming expensive, so I will sell tomato
and buy potato
so that balance remains"

Result:

  • You are left with fewer tomatoes
  • More potatoes

  • 20 × ₹10 = ₹200

  • 10 potatoes × ₹10 = ₹100

This is Impermanent Loss

You didn't lose anything,
but you earned less compared to just HODLing

🔄 Why is it called "Impermanent"?

If tomato's price comes back to ₹10:

  • That's why "impermanent"
  • Once you withdraw → it becomes permanent

⭐ Price is decided by LP (Liquidity Provider) — NOT Uniswap.

Uniswap itself sets no price.
The first person who adds liquidity sets the price.

🔥 REAL EXPLANATION (Super Simple)

💡 When the first LP creates the pool, they must give ETH + TOKEN in some ratio.

Example:

1 ETH + 1000 BAN

Then price automatically becomes:

price = tokenReserve / ethReserve price = 1000 / 1 = 1000 BAN per ETH

Uniswap decided nothing.
The one who created the pool decided it.

⭐ "WHY? Why does LP decide the price?"

Because:

  • Uniswap V1 = AMM (Automated Market Maker)
  • Smart contract only follows the formula: x * y = k
  • Initial reserves given by LP → formula derives price from that
In simple terms:
"Whatever rate the first goods are kept in the shop at, that becomes the starting price."

⭐ IMPORTANT — Price = Ratio, Not a fixed number

Price = Token Reserve ÷ ETH Reserve

If the first LP gives:

2 ETH 2000 BAN

Then price is still:

2000 / 2 = 1000 BAN per ETH

If someone accidentally gives:

1 ETH 500 BAN

Then price will become:

500 BAN per ETH

Meaning price is LP's responsibility.

❗ What if LP sets the wrong price?

Very important:

If an LP makes a HUGE mistake, like:

1 ETH + 200 BAN (price = 200 BAN per ETH instead of 1000)

What will happen?

  • 💡 Arbitrage traders will immediately buy cheap BAN from the pool and sell in expensive market.
  • 💡 Until the price is not equal to global market, arbitrage will keep happening.
  • 💡 LP will get impermanent loss.
In simple terms:
"If you set the wrong price, market people will immediately loot your shop and bring it to the correct price."

⭐ Then who maintains the price after that?

TRADERS maintain the price.

  • If BAN is cheap → traders buy → token reserve decreases → price up
  • If BAN is expensive → traders sell → token reserve increases → price down

Price automatically becomes equal to market.

Exact output = shopkeeper must guarantee he gives you exactly 5 bananas
NO MATTER HOW BAD THE PRICE SLIPPAGE IS.

That is dangerous for shop.

So he does this:

💡 He charges EXTRA — to protect himself from losing too much stock.

  • — LPs get extra benefit
  • — Pool protected
